# Online Retail Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering

## đź“Ś Business Problem & Objective
In e-commerce, treating all customers equally leads to inefficient marketing spend and lower retention rates. This project applies machine learning to a transactional dataset containing over 360,000 entries to compress chaotic raw transaction logs into distinct, actionable customer personas. 

By applying the **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary Value)** framework and **K-Means Clustering**, we segment the customer base into distinct groups, enabling marketing teams to deploy hyper-targeted retention and acquisition campaigns.

## đź“Š Workflow Execution

### 1. Data Cleaning & Anomaly Detection
* Handled missing records by filtering out rows with missing `CustomerID` designations.
* Eliminated system data anomalies, including negative quantities (order cancellations/returns) and products with a `UnitPrice` of zero (promotions/administrative errors).
* Removed duplicate records to ensure absolute transactional integrity.

### 2. RFM Feature Engineering
Transformed raw invoice rows into a unique customer-centric dataset by engineering three critical business metrics:
* **Recency (R):** Number of days between the customer's last purchase and a synthetic dataset endpoint snapshot date.
* **Frequency (F):** Total number of unique orders placed by the customer.
* **Monetary (M):** Total historical revenue generated by the customer.

### 3. Log Transformation & Scaling
Because e-commerce data inherently contains massive outliers (wholesale clients/corporate buyers), a **Log Transformation** (`np.log1p`) was executed to normalize the heavily skewed distributions, followed by `StandardScaler` optimization to ensure uniform geometric weight during distance calculations.

### 4. Mathematical Optimization (The Elbow Method)
Using Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (Inertia), the mathematical "elbow" point was identified at **$k = 3$**, representing the optimal number of clusters for this population.
